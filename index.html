Hello. I am Brian. I am amidst a career pivot towards data analytics. My passion for data comes from a curiousity to know what is knowable and a keen desire to effectively problem-solve. I spent the last 2 years knee-deep in grocery store ecommerce data. In the role of supervisor, it quickly became apparent there were metrics other than KPI, that drove KPI goal adherence. In other words, the business problem was not properly defined. Beforehand, I spent 21 years in an analytical chemistry department focused on environmental remediation. Data was compiled at instrument and then warehoused on a LIMS. This is a world of control charts, 6 degrees of freedom and ad hoc problem-solving. In my final 3 years in the lab, I served as the operational supervisor. In this role I was introduced to formal problem-solving tools which were an ephiphany. I cultivated my problem-skills with formal six sigma training. I am entirely comfortable with reporting to all levels of management and presenting in public. I consider distilling information a strength of mine and have set about honing the skills needed for increasingly complex analytical tasks. What I have learned in the last 2 months as a fully dedicated data analyst is, apart from a properly defined business problem (which includes stakeholder agreement on the definition and scope!), cleaning data is equally important. It is not as straight-forward as I might have initially thought and requires failure mode and effects type analysis. Poorly cleaned data creates rework and reprocessing loops which cost the business time and money. I want to bring my creative and analytical energies to a team that is willing to think outside the box a little with their next data analyst hire. I will not waste your time pretending to besomething I am not. Details and deadlines are not optional.
