Hello. I am Brian. I am amidst a career pivot towards data analytics. My passion for data comes from a curiosity to know what is knowable and a keen desire to effectively problem-solve. I spent the last 2 years knee-deep in grocery store e-commerce data. In the role of supervisor, it quickly became apparent there were metrics other than KPI that drove KPI goal adherence. In other words, the business problem was not properly defined. Beforehand, I spent 21 years in an analytical chemistry department focused on environmental remediation. Data was compiled at instrument and then warehoused in a LIMS — a world of control charts, rigorous quality standards, and ad hoc problem-solving. In my final 3 years in the lab, I served as the operational supervisor. In this role I was introduced to formal problem-solving tools, which were an epiphany. I cultivated my problem-solving skills with formal Six Sigma training. I am entirely comfortable reporting to all levels of management and presenting in public. I consider distilling information a strength and have set about honing the skills needed for increasingly complex analytical tasks. What I have learned in the last 2 months as a fully dedicated data analyst is that, apart from a properly defined business problem — which includes stakeholder agreement on definition and scope — cleaning data is equally important. It is not as straightforward as I initially thought and requires failure mode and effects type analysis. Poorly cleaned data creates rework and reprocessing loops that cost the business time and money. I want to bring my creative and analytical energies to a team willing to think outside the box a little with their next data analyst hire. I will not waste your time pretending to be something I am not. Details and deadlines are not optional.
